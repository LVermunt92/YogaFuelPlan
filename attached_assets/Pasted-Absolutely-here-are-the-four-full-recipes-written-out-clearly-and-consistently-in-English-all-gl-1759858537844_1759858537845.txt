🥦 1. Balanced Beetroot Broccoli Bowl

A nourishing, balanced meal with beetroot hummus, broccoli steaks, and a zesty vinaigrette.

Ingredients

For the beetroot hummus
	•	1 small beetroot (boiled or roasted, peeled)
	•	250 g cooked chickpeas (with 1 tbsp of their liquid)
	•	2 tbsp tahini
	•	½ tsp cumin
	•	1 small garlic clove
	•	Juice of ½ lemon
	•	1 ice cube
	•	Salt and pepper, to taste

For the broccoli
	•	½ large broccoli, cut into 1 thick “steak”
	•	1 tbsp olive oil
	•	Salt and pepper

For the vinaigrette
	•	1½ tbsp extra virgin olive oil
	•	1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
	•	½ tsp chopped red chilli
	•	1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
	•	½ tsp agave syrup (optional)

For serving
	•	100 g tempeh (or 120 g grilled chicken breast)
	•	1 slice gluten-free sourdough or 80 g cooked quinoa
	•	Side salad: rocket (arugula), cucumber, mint, lemon juice

Method
	1.	Cook the beetroot until tender (boil or roast, about 30 min), then peel.
	2.	Make the hummus: blend beetroot, chickpeas, tahini, cumin, garlic, lemon juice, and chickpea water. Add the ice cube while blending. Season with salt and pepper until smooth.
	3.	Prepare the broccoli: heat olive oil in a pan and sear the broccoli steak for 2–3 minutes per side until browned and tender.
	4.	Cook the protein: pan-fry tempeh or chicken in olive oil with a little cumin, salt, and pepper.
	5.	Make the vinaigrette: whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, chilli, parsley, and agave.
	6.	Assemble: spread hummus on the plate, top with broccoli and protein, drizzle with vinaigrette, and serve with the salad and bread or quinoa.

🍅 2. Mediterranean Variation

Fresh, herby and bright — with roasted red pepper hummus instead of beetroot.

Ingredients
	•	1 red bell pepper, roasted and peeled
	•	250 g cooked chickpeas (with 1 tbsp liquid)
	•	2 tbsp tahini
	•	½ tsp smoked paprika
	•	1 small garlic clove
	•	Juice of ½ lemon
	•	1 ice cube
	•	Salt and pepper
	•	½ broccoli, cut into steaks
	•	100 g grilled tempeh or tofu
	•	1 tbsp olive oil

Vinaigrette
	•	1½ tbsp extra virgin olive oil
	•	1 tbsp red wine vinegar
	•	1 tbsp chopped basil and oregano
	•	Pinch of chilli flakes

To serve
	•	80 g cooked quinoa or millet
	•	Side of cherry tomatoes and olives

Method
	1.	Blend roasted pepper, chickpeas, tahini, paprika, garlic, lemon juice, and chickpea water until smooth.
	2.	Sear broccoli steaks in olive oil.
	3.	Grill tempeh or tofu.
	4.	Mix vinaigrette ingredients.
	5.	Plate the hummus, top with broccoli and protein, drizzle vinaigrette, and serve with quinoa and the tomato-olive mix.

🧆 3. Middle Eastern Variation

Earthy and aromatic with za’atar and a touch of dukkah crunch.

Ingredients
	•	1 small beetroot, cooked and peeled
	•	250 g cooked chickpeas
	•	2 tbsp tahini
	•	½ tsp cumin
	•	1 garlic clove
	•	Juice of ½ lemon
	•	1 ice cube
	•	Salt and pepper
	•	½ broccoli, sliced into steaks
	•	100 g tempeh or 120 g grilled chicken
	•	1 tbsp olive oil

Toppings
	•	½ tsp za’atar
	•	1 tsp dukkah (optional)
	•	Fresh parsley and mint

To serve
	•	80 g cooked quinoa tabbouleh (quinoa, parsley, tomato, lemon, olive oil)

Method
	1.	Blend hummus ingredients as above.
	2.	Pan-sear broccoli and protein.
	3.	Sprinkle za’atar over broccoli while warm.
	4.	Serve hummus with broccoli and protein, sprinkle with dukkah, parsley, and mint.
	5.	Add a side of quinoa tabbouleh.

🍠 4. Warm Winter Version

A cozy, roasted variation with sweet potatoes and warm tahini-lemon dressing.

Ingredients
	•	1 small beetroot, cooked
	•	250 g cooked chickpeas
	•	2 tbsp tahini
	•	½ tsp cumin
	•	1 garlic clove
	•	Juice of ½ lemon
	•	1 ice cube
	•	Salt and pepper
	•	½ broccoli, cut into steaks
	•	150 g roasted sweet potato cubes
	•	100 g baked tempeh or tofu
	•	Olive oil

Warm Tahini Dressing
	•	1 tbsp tahini
	•	1 tbsp lemon juice
	•	1 tbsp warm water
	•	1 tsp olive oil
	•	Pinch of salt

Method
	1.	Make the beetroot hummus as before.
	2.	Roast sweet potato cubes in olive oil at 200°C for 25 minutes.
	3.	Pan-sear broccoli steaks and protein.
	4.	Whisk warm tahini dressing until smooth.
	5.	Serve hummus with roasted sweet potato and broccoli, drizzle dressing on top.
