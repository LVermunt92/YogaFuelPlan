Here is the 1-serving tofu + leek version.

⸻

🍽️ Ingredients (1 serving)
	•	100–125 g dried Wieringer beans
	•	or ± 200–250 g cooked beans (from jar/can, rinsed)
	•	125–150 g firm tofu, pressed and patted dry (1 tofu “steak”)
	•	1 small leek (white and light green parts), in thin rings
	•	½ small onion, finely chopped
	•	1–2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
	•	200 g chopped tomatoes or passata (about ½ can) or 200 g fresh tomatoes, diced
	•	½–1 tbsp tomato paste (optional)
	•	¼–½ tsp chili flakes or cayenne pepper
	•	1 small sprig fresh herbs (e.g. thyme or oregano) or a pinch of dried herbs
	•	250–375 ml vegetable stock (or water + stock powder)
	•	Olive oil (or neutral oil) for frying / sautéing
	•	Salt and freshly ground black pepper
	•	Optional: a small splash of lemon or lime juice
	•	¼–½ tsp sumac, for finishing
	•	Optional: a little extra olive oil for serving

⸻

🍲 Preparation
	1.	Cook the beans
	•	If using dried beans: soak 8–12 hours, drain, rinse, then cook in fresh water (about 3 cm above the beans) for 45–60 minutes until tender. Do not add salt while cooking.
	•	Drain and keep some cooking liquid aside.
	2.	Make the chili-tomato bean stew
	•	Heat a little olive oil in a pan.
	•	Sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
	•	Add the leek and cook until it softens.
	•	Add tomatoes, tomato paste (if using), chili flakes and herbs. Let simmer 1–2 minutes.
	•	Add the beans and enough stock to just cover.
	•	Simmer on low heat for about 15–20 minutes so the flavors combine.
	•	Season with salt and pepper. If the sauce is too thick, loosen with a bit of stock or bean cooking liquid.
	3.	Prepare the tofu
	•	Heat a grill pan or frying pan with a little oil over medium-high heat.
	•	Season the tofu with salt and pepper.
	•	Fry about 3–4 minutes per side, until browned and slightly crispy.
	4.	Serve
	•	Spoon the bean-tomato stew into a deep plate.
	•	Place the tofu on top.
	•	Finish with sumac and, if desired, a drizzle of olive oil and/or a splash of lemon or lime juice.
